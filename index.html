Fractions

A fraction represents a part of a whole or a division of something into equal parts.

"Fraction" word traces its origin from the root Latin word "Fractus", which means "broken".

Parts of Fraction:
Consider a/b as a fraction, the upper part (a) represents the numerator and the lower part (b) represents the denominator.

A fractional bar (—) is a horizontal line that separates the numerator and denominator in a fraction, such as 107(numerator = 10, denominator = 7).

Types of fractions

  1. Proper Fractions: A proper fraction is one where the numerator (top number) is less than the denominator (bottom number): "numerator < denominator".
    Example: 2/5
  2. Improper fractions: An improper fraction is one where the numerator equals or exceeds the denominator.“numerator > denominator”
    Example: 11/7
  3. Like Fractions: Like fractions, as the name implies, are fractions that are similar or identical. Alternatively, these are the fractions with the same denominator.
    Example: 3/10 and 7/10.
  4. Unlike Fractions: Unlike fractions are ones that are dissimilar. Fractions with distinct denominators.
    Example: 8/5 and 3/7.
  5. Unit Fractions: When the numerator equals one, the fraction is referred to as a unit.
    Example: 1/6

Comparing Like fractions :

When comparing fractions with the same denominator, it is easier to identify which fraction is greater or smaller.

After determining whether the denominators are the same, we can simply search for the fraction with the larger numerator.

If the numerators and denominators are equal, so are the fractions.
